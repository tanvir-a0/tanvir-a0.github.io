Intelligent Firefighting Robot

An end-to-end remote suppression platform engineered to keep first responders out of harm's way while delivering situational awareness, precision actuation, and thermal resilience.

Design & Engineering

Integrated drive modules, motor controllers, sensors, and power delivery were selected to tolerate high power needed environment. The outer layer is then cover in a temperature resistant material. This was just a prototype for much larger project.

Precision CAD Workflow

Every subsystem was validated in Fusion 360 before fabrication, enabling compatibility checks between the systems, validation of fit and function, and fast iteration without wasting material.

Human-Centered Control

A CustomTkinter desktop console provides real-time status, direct images from the robot, manual overrides, and diagnostics. I tried to mimic the layout of a modern mission control system, simple and smooth.

Mission Overview

The Intelligent Firefighting Robot is a large-format, teleoperated platform built to move through hazardous zones, deliver high-volume suppression, and stream live situational data back to incident command.

It fuses mechanical design, embedded electronics, and software into a cohesive response system capable of real-time firefighting operations without exposing humans to extreme conditions.

Prototype Scope

This build is a functional proof of concept validated in controlled test burns. It demonstrates feasibility and informs future ruggedization, autonomy, and certification work.

From concept to deployment demos, I owned CAD, fabrication, electronics integration, control software, and wireless comms — shaping a full-stack robotics experience end to end.

Subsystem Engineering

Each critical subsystem was authored digitally in CAD, validated to see weather it will fit in the robot or not, then applied that knowledge in physical robot.

High-Torque Drive Motor

High-Torque motor was used to drive the robot. This motor is powerful enough to carry 10 liters of water/fire suppression liquid inside the robot. All these parameters ware calculated during the robot design process.

Suppression Pump Module

Another important consideration during the desing was setting/placing the pump and then routing the cables. All these ware done in Fusion 360 before the fabrication process.

Electrical Systems

Other electronics ware also designed and tested in Fuision 360 and then fabricated. The challange for Electrical system as to ensure that they are kept away from fire while making sure they can release heat efficiently.

Key Capabilities

  • Teleoperated firefighting platform for hazardous environment and remote suppression.
  • End-to-end software stack handling motion control, telemetry, and visual feedback.
  • Custom electronics includes integrating actuators, sensing, and power distribution.
  • CAD-to-fabrication design process ensures structural precision.
  • Fully wireless communication using Wi-Fi.
